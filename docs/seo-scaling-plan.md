## 1. SEO Strategy Overview

### This Is BOFU-First SEO

We rank for queries where the searcher is ready to make a decision:

- "cigna global vs allianz care"
- "best health insurance for expats in spain"
- "img global review"
- "health insurance for digital nomads in portugal"

We do NOT rank for:

- "what is health insurance"
- "how to move abroad"
- "expat lifestyle tips"

### This Is NOT a Content Strategy

There is no blog. There is no editorial calendar. There are no "thought leadership" pieces.

Every page exists because it answers a specific decision a buyer needs to make. If a page doesn't help someone choose between options, it doesn't exist.

### Success Definition

Success is measured by:

1. **Impressions** in commercial queries (GSC)
2. **Rankings** in low-KD decision queries (positions 1-10)
3. **Conversion readiness** (pages that can monetize)

We do not optimize for:

- Traffic volume
- Time on site
- Social shares
- "Brand awareness"

## 2. Core Scaling Logic (The Engine)

### The Multiplication Model

Pages scale through structured combinations of:

| Dimension | Count | Examples |
|-----------|-------|----------|
| Countries | 30+ | Spain, Portugal, Mexico, Thailand, UAE, Germany... |
| Providers | 8-12 | Cigna, Allianz, Bupa, IMG, Aetna, GeoBlue... |
| Personas | 6-8 | Retirees, families, digital nomads, students, freelancers... |
| Comparisons | N×(N-1)/2 | Cigna vs Allianz, Bupa vs IMG... |
| Constraints | 5-10 | Pre-existing conditions, US coverage, maternity... |
| Regions | 5-8 | Southeast Asia, Western Europe, Latin America... |

### The Math

```
Base pages:
- 30 countries × 1 insurance page = 30 pages
- 30 countries × 10 providers = 300 pages
- 10 providers × 1 review = 10 pages
- 10 providers → 45 comparisons
- 6 personas × 1 page = 6 pages
- 6 personas × 30 countries = 180 pages
- 5 regions × 1 page = 5 pages
- 5 constraint pages = 5 pages

Subtotal: ~580 pages

Second-order combinations:
- 30 countries × 6 personas = 180 pages
- 30 countries × 45 comparisons = 1,350 pages (subset)
- 10 providers × 6 personas = 60 pages
- 5 regions × 10 providers = 50 pages
- 5 regions × 6 personas = 30 pages

Total addressable: 3,000+ pages
```

The key: Every page is a unique decision context, not a keyword variation.

## 3. Page Type Hierarchy

### Priority Order (Build First → Build Later)

| Priority | Page Type | URL Pattern | Purpose | EPC Potential |
|----------|-----------|-------------|---------|---------------|
| 1 | Compare | `/compare/[a]-vs-[b]` | Direct conversion | Highest |
| 2 | Country+Provider | `/countries/[country]/insurance/[provider]` | Localized decision | High |
| 3 | Provider Reviews | `/providers/[provider]` | Trust building | High |
| 4 | Best Of | `/best/[category]` | Aggregated recommendations | High |
| 5 | Country Insurance | `/countries/[country]/insurance` | Country entry point | Medium |
| 6 | Persona | `/insurance-for/[persona]` | Audience entry point | Medium |
| 7 | Guides | `/guides/[topic]` | Decision support | Low |

### Page Type Details

#### /compare/ (Priority 1)
- Highest commercial intent
- Direct A/B decision
- Build these FIRST after core infrastructure
- Target: `[provider] vs [provider]` queries
- Example: `/compare/cigna-global-vs-allianz-care`

#### /countries/[country]/insurance/[provider] (Priority 2)
- Localized provider pages
- "cigna global spain" queries
- Unique content: local network, visa compliance, local costs
- Example: `/countries/spain/insurance/cigna-global`

#### /providers/[provider] (Priority 3)
- Comprehensive provider reviews
- Trust signals (ratings, pros/cons)
- Links out to all comparisons and country pages
- Example: `/providers/cigna-global`

#### /best/[category] (Priority 4)
- Curated recommendations
- "best expat insurance for [X]" queries
- Strong internal linking hub
- Example: `/best/expat-health-insurance`

#### /countries/[country]/insurance (Priority 5)
- Country entry point
- Healthcare system context
- Links to all providers available in that country
- Example: `/countries/spain/insurance`

#### /insurance-for/[persona] (Priority 6)
- Persona-specific recommendations
- "health insurance for digital nomads" queries
- Links to relevant providers and countries
- Example: `/insurance-for/digital-nomads`

#### /guides/[topic] (Priority 7 — Limited)
- ONLY decision-support content
- Deductibles, claims, pre-existing conditions
- NOT general education
- Cap at 10-15 guides total
- Example: `/guides/choosing-deductibles`

## 5. Internal Linking Strategy (The Moat)

### Why Internal Linking > Backlinks Early

1. We control it completely
2. It distributes authority to new pages immediately
3. It signals topic relationships to Google
4. It creates the "engine" that scales

Backlinks matter later. Internal linking matters now.

### Linking Rules

#### From Country Pages
```
Country → Provider reviews for that country
Country → Best-of relevant to that country
Country → Comparisons between providers in that country
Country → Personas relevant to that country
```

#### From Provider Pages
```
Provider → All comparisons involving that provider
Provider → All countries where provider operates
Provider → Personas that provider serves well
Provider → Best-of pages where provider is featured
```

#### From Comparison Pages
```
Comparison → Both provider pages
Comparison → Countries where both operate
Comparison → Best-of page for the category
Comparison → Related comparisons (same providers)
```

#### From Best-Of Pages
```
Best-of → All featured provider pages
Best-of → Top comparison pages
Best-of → Relevant country pages
Best-of → Related persona pages
```

#### From Persona Pages
```
Persona → Recommended providers for that persona
Persona → Recommended countries for that persona
Persona → Best-of page for that persona
Persona → Relevant guides
```

### No Orphan Pages

Every page must have:
- At least 3 internal links pointing TO it
- At least 3 internal links pointing FROM it
- A logical place in the site hierarchy

If a page can't meet these criteria, don't build it yet.

### Link Placement

- Contextual links within body content (highest value)
- Related links sidebar (PageShell component)
- Comparison tables with provider links
- "See also" sections at page bottom

## 6. Content & Structural Guardrails

### Every Page Answers a Decision

Before creating a page, answer: "What decision does this help someone make?"

- ✅ "Should I choose Cigna or Allianz for Spain?" → Build it
- ✅ "What's the best insurance for retirees in Portugal?" → Build it
- ❌ "What is international health insurance?" → Don't build it
- ❌ "10 tips for living abroad" → Don't build it

### Tables > Prose

Comparison data belongs in tables, not paragraphs.

```
Good:
| Provider | Deductible | Monthly Cost | Annual Max |
|----------|------------|--------------|------------|
| Cigna    | $0-10,000  | $150-400     | Unlimited  |
| Allianz  | $0-5,000   | $130-350     | $2M        |

Bad:
Cigna offers deductibles ranging from zero to ten thousand dollars,
with monthly costs between one hundred fifty and four hundred dollars...
```

### Structural Uniqueness > Word Count

A page with:
- Unique data table
- Specific provider + country combination
- Clear decision framework

Is more valuable than 2,000 words of generic insurance advice.

### No Generic Education

We don't explain:
- What insurance is
- Why you need insurance
- How healthcare works generally

We explain:
- Which insurance to choose
- How these specific options compare
- What this means for YOUR situation

### No Lifestyle Content

We don't cover:
- Best restaurants in Barcelona
- Cost of living comparisons
- Visa application walkthroughs
- "Expat life" content

We cover:
- Insurance decisions for people in those places

### No TOFU Without BOFU Anchors

Never build top-of-funnel content unless it links directly to bottom-of-funnel pages that can convert.

Example: A guide on "pre-existing conditions" only exists because it links to:
- Provider pages that cover pre-existing conditions
- Comparison pages for those providers
- Best-of pages for people with pre-existing conditions

## 7. Indexing & GSC Strategy

### Controlled Batch Submission

Don't submit everything at once. Submit in batches:

1. **Batch 1 (Day 1):** Homepage, main hub pages (5-10 pages)
2. **Batch 2 (Day 3):** Provider pages, best-of pages (10-15 pages)
3. **Batch 3 (Day 7):** Country pages (20-30 pages)
4. **Batch 4 (Day 14):** Comparison pages (20-30 pages)
5. **Batch 5 (Day 21):** Remaining pages

### Index Priority Order

1. Homepage
2. /best/expat-health-insurance (main money page)
3. Provider pages
4. Top 6 country pages
5. Comparison pages
6. Persona pages
7. Guides
8. Everything else

### What to Monitor

**First 30 days:**
- Pages indexed (target: 100%)
- Crawl errors (target: 0)
- Mobile usability issues (target: 0)

**30-90 days:**
- Impressions (growth week-over-week)
- Queries appearing (diversity)
- Average position (improvement trend)

**90+ days:**
- Click-through rate
- Position for target queries
- Pages ranking in top 10

### When to Expand vs Pause

**Expand aggressively when:**
- Indexation rate > 90%
- Impressions growing
- No quality warnings
- Pages ranking for intended queries

**Pause and diagnose when:**
- Indexation rate < 70%
- Impressions flat or declining
- Quality warnings appear
- Pages not ranking for any queries after 60 days

## 8. What We Do NOT Do (Anti-Strategy)

### No Blog Calendar

There is no weekly publishing schedule. Pages are built when they serve a decision need, not to maintain "freshness."

### No Topical Authority Play

We don't try to rank for "insurance" by covering every insurance topic. We rank for expat health insurance decisions by being the best resource for those specific decisions.

### No News, Trends, or Lifestyle Content

- ❌ "2026 Travel Trends for Expats"
- ❌ "Why More People Are Moving Abroad"
- ❌ "Interview with an Expat in Thailand"
- ❌ "Best Cafes for Remote Workers in Lisbon"

### No Keyword Stuffing

Pages target decision contexts, not keyword density. If a page reads unnaturally, it's wrong.

### No Chasing Head Terms Early

We don't try to rank for:
- "health insurance"
- "expat insurance"
- "international insurance"

We rank for:
- "cigna global vs allianz spain"
- "best health insurance for retirees in portugal"
- "img global review 2026"

Head terms come later (if ever) through accumulated authority, not direct targeting.

### No Backlink Schemes

No:
- Guest posting for links
- Link exchanges
- PBNs
- Paid links

Yes:
- Natural links from being useful
- Digital PR if relevant
- Resource page inclusions (organic)

### No Social Media Strategy

Social exists for basic presence only. It is not a traffic channel. No resources allocated to social growth.

## Quick Reference: Decision Tree

```
Should I build this page?
│
├─ Does it help someone make a buying decision?
│  ├─ Yes → Continue
│  └─ No → DON'T BUILD
│
├─ Is it a unique decision context?
│  ├─ Yes → Continue
│  └─ No (duplicate of existing page) → DON'T BUILD
│
├─ Can I link TO it from 3+ existing pages?
│  ├─ Yes → Continue
│  └─ No → Build linking pages first, OR DON'T BUILD
│
├─ Can I link FROM it to 3+ existing pages?
│  ├─ Yes → Continue
│  └─ No → Build destination pages first, OR DON'T BUILD
│
└─ BUILD IT
```
