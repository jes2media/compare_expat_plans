# Editorial Guidelines

This document defines the writing style, tone, and content standards for Compare Expat Plans.

## Voice & Tone

### Be Direct
Get to the point. Readers are here for information, not entertainment.

- **Do:** "A deductible is the amount you pay before insurance kicks in."
- **Don't:** "You might be wondering what exactly a deductible is. Well, let us explain this important concept to you!"

### Be Honest
Acknowledge trade-offs. We're not selling insurance — we're helping people make informed decisions.

- **Do:** "Budget plans work well for healthy people who rarely use healthcare. If you have ongoing conditions, comprehensive coverage may actually save money."
- **Don't:** "This amazing plan is perfect for everyone!"

### Be Practical
Give actionable information. Tell readers what to do, not just what to know.

- **Do:** "File claims within 30 days while details are fresh."
- **Don't:** "It's generally advisable to submit claims in a timely manner."

### Be Conversational (But Professional)
Write like you're explaining something to a smart friend — not lecturing or being overly casual.

- **Do:** "Here's the thing: travel insurance and health insurance aren't the same."
- **Don't:** "It is imperative that consumers understand the distinction between these two product categories."
- **Don't:** "So like, travel insurance is totally different, you know?"

## Writing Standards

### Address the Reader Directly
Use "you" and "your." Make it personal.

- **Do:** "If you're planning to have children abroad..."
- **Don't:** "Expats who are planning to have children abroad..."

### Use Short Paragraphs
2-4 sentences max. White space helps readability.

### Use Bullet Points and Lists
Break up complex information. Make content scannable.

### Use Specific Numbers
Concrete details build trust.

- **Do:** "Premiums typically range from $100-300/month"
- **Don't:** "Premiums vary depending on many factors"

### Acknowledge Uncertainty
Use hedging language when appropriate: "typically," "often," "may," "usually," "can."

- **Do:** "Most plans have waiting periods of 10-12 months"
- **Don't:** "All plans have 12-month waiting periods" (unless literally true)

### Avoid Superlatives and Hype
No "best ever," "amazing," "incredible," "game-changing."

- **Do:** "Cigna Global has a large provider network"
- **Don't:** "Cigna Global has an incredible, industry-leading network"

### Show Both Sides
Every option has pros and cons. Present them.

```
**What we like:** Large global network, good customer service
**Considerations:** Premiums can be higher than competitors
```

## Content Structure

### Page Titles
Clear and descriptive. Include the topic and year for "best of" content.

- "Best Expat Health Insurance 2026"
- "How to File International Health Insurance Claims"
- "Pre-Existing Conditions: What Expats Need to Know"

### Headlines (H1)
Expand on the title. Tell readers what they'll learn.

- "Best International Health Insurance for Families"
- "Finding the right coverage for your family abroad means balancing maternity benefits, pediatric care, and budget."

### Section Headings (H2, H3)
Be specific. Headings should tell readers what's in the section.

- **Do:** "What Happens If Your Claim Is Denied"
- **Don't:** "Important Information"

### Introductions
Get to the point in the first paragraph. No lengthy preambles.

### Comparison Tables
Use tables to compare options side-by-side. Include:
- Clear column headers
- Consistent data types
- Meaningful comparisons

### CTAs (Calls to Action)
Be helpful, not pushy.

- **Do:** "Compare plans to see actual prices for your situation"
- **Don't:** "Act now! Don't miss out!"

### FAQs
Answer real questions people have. Be concise but complete.

### Disclaimers
Include where appropriate. Be clear we're not insurance brokers.

Standard disclaimer:
> **Disclaimer:** This guide is for informational purposes only. Coverage details and pricing vary by provider and policy. We are not insurance brokers. Always read policy documents carefully and verify information with providers.

## Words to Avoid

| Avoid | Use Instead |
|-------|-------------|
| Amazing, incredible, awesome | (just describe the feature) |
| Cheap | Affordable, budget-friendly |
| Best (without qualification) | Top-rated, highly rated, our pick |
| Simple, easy (if it's not) | Straightforward, manageable |
| Just (minimizing) | (remove it) |
| Very, really, extremely | (usually unnecessary) |
| Utilize | Use |
| In order to | To |
| At this point in time | Now |

## Words to Use

- Clear, direct verbs: covers, includes, costs, requires, offers
- Hedging when honest: typically, often, may, usually, can
- Reader-focused: you, your, you'll

## Formatting

### Bold
Use for key terms, important points, and labels in lists.

### Links
Link to related content naturally within text. Use descriptive anchor text.

- **Do:** "Learn more about [choosing your deductible](/guides/choosing-deductibles)"
- **Don't:** "[Click here](/guides/choosing-deductibles) to learn more"

### Numbers
- Spell out one through nine
- Use numerals for 10+
- Always use numerals for money, percentages, and measurements
- Use ranges with en-dash: $100–300, 10–12 months

## Examples

### Good Opening
> A deductible is the amount you pay out of pocket before your insurance starts covering costs. If you have a $1,000 deductible, you'll pay the first $1,000 of your medical expenses each year. After that, your insurance kicks in.

### Good Comparison
> **Choose a lower deductible if:**
> - You have ongoing health conditions
> - You use healthcare services regularly
> - You prefer predictable monthly costs
>
> **Choose a higher deductible if:**
> - You're generally healthy
> - You rarely see doctors
> - You can afford unexpected medical costs

### Good Honest Assessment
> IMG Global works for families who need basic international coverage at a lower price point.
>
> **Family strengths:**
> - Lowest premiums for family coverage
> - High deductible options reduce costs further
>
> **Considerations:** Limited maternity benefits. Fewer wellness programs. Better suited for families without complex health needs.
